Project Work.

Absorptive Removal of chromium from Aqueous phase using treated sawdust. (July 2018-June 2019)

We removed chromium ions from aqueous phase (A synthetic Chromium solution made with K2Cr2O7 dissolved in 1 litre water) using treated sawdust. Sawdust was converted into charcoal by specific processes as follows:-

  • Carbonization: Cleaned sawdust is passed through a sieve and heated in a furnace (covered) at 500 degrees Celsius for 90 minutes.
  • Activation: Resultant charcoal is taken and activated with concentrated H2SO4 and Na2CO3. After that we put the mixture into the furnace for 30 minutes for 600 degrees Celsius.
  • Neutralization: The resultant activated charcoal was taken out of furnace and neutralized with a dilute NaOH solution and dried in hot air oven

With this activated carbon we have done studies like Adsorption Isotherms, effect of pH, kinetic studies and change of concentration with adsorbent dosage and made following conclusions.

  1. Follows both Langmuir and Freundlich isotherms
  2. Adsorption increases with decrease in pH.
  3. Follows both pseudo 1st and 2nd order characteristics
